About Habitat

Habitat for Humanity Ireland was founded in 2002. It is a non-profit organisation, working on behalf of and in partnership with other Habitat for Humanity offices throughout the world. Habitat Ireland has key partnerships with various countries including India, Zambia, Romania, Cambodia and of course Malawi. Habitat is also working in Ireland to provide affordable homes for families in need. They focus upon helping local people and those across the world. This local- global link is something that I find impressive and admirable. They are dedicated to eliminating substandard housing worldwide and to making adequate, affordable shelter a matter of conscience and action. Habitat are engaged in sustainable development which gives families a hand up, not a hand out. The families receiving homes, often build alongside Habitat volunteers so that they are engaged with the process. Habitat educates Malawian families on topics such as HIV and Malaria prevention. Additionally, Habitat provide training on will writing which is hugely important in Malawi as women don’t have property rights and are often the most vulnerable in their community.

What they do?

Their vision: A world where everyone has a decent place to live.

Their mission: Habitat for Humanity brings people together to build strength, stability and self-reliance through shelter.

Their principles and values

Focus on Shelter:They believe in creating opportunities for all people to live in decent, durable shelter by helping to build, renovate or preserve homes, and by partnering with others to accelerate and broaden access to affordable housing as a foundation for breaking the cycle of poverty. Advocate for Affordable Housing: They believe in promoting decent, affordable housing for all, and supporting the global community’s commitment to housing as a basic human right. We advocate for just and fair housing policy to eliminate the constraints that contribute to poverty housing. Promote Dignity and Hope: They believe that no one lives in dignity until everyone can live in dignity. We believe that every person has something to contribute and something to gain from creating communities in which all people have decent affordable places to live. We believe that dignity and hope are best achieved through equitable, accountable partnerships. Support Sustainable and Transformational Development: They view our work as successful when it transforms lives, and promotes positive and lasting social and economic growth within a community, when it is based on mutual trust and fully shared accomplishment, and when it demonstrates responsible stewardship of all resources entrusted to us.

Who they help

Habitat for Humnanity Ireland is part of a an international effort by partnered Habitat For Humanity organsiations all over the world. Since 1976, Habitat partnerships and their volunteers have helped more than 9.8 million people access their basic human right to a safe, decent place to live.

With the help of generous donors, Habitat Ireland can help people in:
  • Vulnerable populations, including orphans, older people, and those affected by HIV/AIDS
  • Single-parent households, often headed by women
  • Marginalised populations
  • Low-income families
